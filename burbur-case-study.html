The Brief

Bold enough to
be remembered.

BUR BUR is a brand that doesn’t fit neatly into a category — and that’s entirely the point. In a sea of clean minimal DTC brands, BUR BUR needed to stand out through personality and confidence, not polish.

The brief: create campaign creative with a visual language so distinct that you’d recognize the brand before you read the name. Warm, bold, unmistakably BUR BUR.

The Strategy

Two statics.
One unmistakable voice.

Unlike brands that need motion to create energy, BUR BUR’s personality is strong enough to carry two static pieces. The ad poster announces. The aesthetic campaign deepens. Together they establish a visual world consistent enough to build brand recall from static alone.

Stage 01

Announce

The ad poster runs cold traffic. Bold composition, warm tones, confident layout. Designed to stop the scroll through sheer visual presence rather than clever copy or motion.

Ad Poster — Feed Static

Stage 02

Deepen

The aesthetic campaign piece retargets warm audiences. Same palette, more atmosphere. By the second touchpoint the brand world is fully established and the viewer already knows what BUR BUR feels like.

Creative Decisions

When personality
is the strategy.

Most brand guidelines exist to create consistency. BUR BUR’s visual language exists to create recognition. There’s a difference — recognition means someone sees the palette and knows who it is before reading the name.

Warm amber and terracotta tones were chosen because they feel human and unpolished in the best possible way. This isn’t a brand that wants to look like a tech startup. It wants to look like something made by people who care about what they make.
