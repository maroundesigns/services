The Brief

A brand built on
what it doesn’t say.

VEIL is a dark luxury fashion brand built around mystery and restraint. The challenge: create a campaign suite that feels editorial — closer to Bottega Veneta than a typical DTC brand — while still functioning as paid social creative.

Most luxury brands fail on social because they try to compete on spectacle. VEIL’s brief was the opposite: communicate exclusivity through silence. Every creative decision had to feel deliberate and withheld.

The Strategy

Three pieces.
One funnel.

Rather than building three independent ads, each piece was designed to play a specific role in a cold-to-warm awareness funnel. The motion piece captures attention, the editorial static builds intrigue, and the campaign ad drives intent.

Stage 01

Capture

Stop the scroll with movement and atmosphere. No product, no price, no CTA — just VEIL’s world rendered in motion.

Typographic Animation — Reels / Stories

Stage 02

Intrigue

The editorial magazine cover communicates brand world and positioning. The viewer wonders what VEIL is before they read the name.

Stage 03

Convert

The Spring Launch campaign ad brings consistent visual language with clear intent — a reason to click, for an audience already primed by stages one and two.

Creative Decisions

Why restraint
is the strategy.

Every luxury brand wants to look premium. Most do it by adding — more visual noise, more copy, bigger logos. VEIL does it by removing. The creative decisions here were almost entirely subtractive.

No product shot in the motion piece. No price in the statics. No discount. No urgency copy. The absence of these conventions signals that VEIL doesn’t need them — which is exactly what luxury positioning requires.

The dark plum and shadow palette was chosen to sit uncomfortably in a feed dominated by bright DTC brands. Discomfort creates pause. Pause creates attention.

Hypothetical Targeting

Who sees this,
and when.

Cold audience targeting would focus on women 22–38 with demonstrated interest in luxury fashion, editorial magazines, niche perfume brands, and high-end lifestyle content. The motion piece runs first — broad, low-cost impressions to build brand familiarity.

Warm retargeting (video viewers 50%+, profile visitors) would then see the Spring Launch campaign ad with a clearer intent signal. The editorial static would run across both phases, acting as a brand anchor.
