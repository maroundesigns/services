The Brief

Sleep apps sell
features. HALO sells
a feeling.

HALO is a premium sleep and wellness app in a crowded category. Most competitors advertise with product screenshots, sleep score graphics, and clinical claims. None of it creates desire.

The brief: make HALO feel like the sleep you want, not the sleep you need. Cinematic, aspirational, intimate. The kind of creative that makes someone stop scrolling at midnight and feel something.

Sleep Deeper — App Ad

The Strategy

Atmosphere first,
download second.

The two-piece strategy leans into the product’s emotional promise. Motion creates the dream state. Static locks in the brand moment. Together they build enough desire to make a download feel like a natural next step rather than a conversion.

Stage 01

Dream

The animated social ad runs top-of-funnel on Reels and Stories. Aurora particles drift over a bedroom scene, headline reveals line by line. No screenshots, no features — pure atmosphere. Goal: make them want the feeling.

Creative Decisions

Aurora as
brand language.

The aurora particle effect was chosen because it’s both scientific and magical — which is exactly where HALO lives. It references the brain’s activity during deep sleep without explaining it. The viewer feels it rather than reads it.

Deep navy backgrounds were chosen over clinical white to position HALO as an experience, not a tool. The difference between a sleep tracker and a sleep sanctuary is entirely in the creative.
